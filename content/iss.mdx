## International Space Station

Date: 1998-11-20

The International Space Station (ISS) is the largest human-made object in space and the longest-occupied habitable artificial satellite in low Earth orbit.
It serves as a microgravity and space environment research laboratory in which scientific research is conducted in astrobiology, astronomy, meteorology, physics, and other fields.

### Specifications
- Length: 73 meters (240 feet)
- Mass: 450,000 kg (1 million lbs)
- Orbit: 400 km (250 miles) above Earth
- Crew capacity: 6-7 astronauts
- First launched: November 20, 1998

### Notable Features
- Modular design with multiple laboratories
- Continuous human presence since 2000
- International collaboration between space agencies
- Regular resupply missions
- Orbiting speed: 27,400 km/h (17,000 mph)
